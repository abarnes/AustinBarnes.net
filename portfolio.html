Vendevor

Ecommerce Platform

July 2012-August 2014

More Info

Overview

Vendevor is an embeddable ecommerce store platform that allows small business owners to easily create their own online stores without the hassle and expense of setting up a traditional e-commerce website. Vendevor includes payment processing at sign up, so you can start selling in minutes.

I was the Chief Technology Officer and lead developer for Vendevor from inception in July 2012 and continued to maintain and expand the platform through August 2014. In addition to building the core platform, I managed mobile app integrations, worked through the PSP (Payment Service Provider) application to process payments for other businesses, and co-raised $490k in funding.

We had over 9,000 sign-ups over $400k in processed transactions. The co-founders and I also won the $25,000 first place prize in the Baylor New Venture Competition in 2013.

Vendevor is currently in negotiations to sell the business to a third party.

Website: vendevor.com. The Github repository for Vendevor is private. Please contact me to view code samples.

Technologies Chosen

CakePHP 2.4 + MySQL - Streamlines development of heavily database-driven applications & compatible with external PHP libraries.
jQuery + jQuery UI - Able to drive advanced UI features with minimal setup and customization.
iOS, Android Apps - Provide a richer and easier-to-use mobile experience for Vendevor customers and encourages switching to Vendevor.
Amazon Web Services - Amazon Elastic Beanstalk coupled with other AWS services allowed us to keep server costs down and respond to spikes in demand.

Third-party Integrations

Authorize.net - Payment Procesing
TransFirst Transaction Central - Payment Processing (as PSP)
Facebook Page Tab Applications - Powered Facebook Fan Page stores
Mailchimp - Email campaign integration
Constant Contact - Email campaign integration
Facebook Graph API - Sharing integrated with Vendevor
Twitter API - Tweeting integrated with Vendevor
Mailgun - Handle automated emails
USPS Shipping Calculator - Provide accurate shipping quotes

FCG Reports

Business Analytics

October 2014-February 2015

More Info

Overview

First Centurion Reports is an internal analytics tool built for First Centurion Group to track revenue, advertising performance, billing rates, bank account transfers, and other key operational metrics for the company's $21 million annual revenue stream. The site integrates with the business CRM, allowing company leaders to easily make business decisions about advertising performance as well as spot problems with banks at a glance, without crunching numbers in excel.

The site contains restricted information and is not publicly available. Screenshots are shown below, and code samples can be provided upon request.

Vendevor CRM

CRM + Email Tool

June 2014

More Info

Overview

When Vendevor hired its first salesman, we needed a customer relationship management system to manage leads and prospects. Salesforce.com partially met our needs but at an unaffordable price for a small startup. I spent two days coding a replacement CRM that met our needs exactly and continues to perform well with over a quarter million leads in the system.

The CRM allows our sales team to view and comment on leads, set up reminders, add leads to various campaigns, and manage email funnels, including integrated email split-testing based on open rates.

Because this is an internal tool, the default CakePHP CSS was used with minimal tweaking.

Technologies Chosen

CakePHP 2.4 + MySQL - Chosen for robust relational database support & familiarity.


Third-party Integrations

Mailgun - Sent automated emails to leads and integrated "open" and "click" tracking


Screenshots

The Vendevor CRM allows sales people to easily filter leads by a wide range of parameters and log all contact with the lead and other pertinent information.
(Yes, it's titled "Salesforce can suck it CRM." We were just a tad irritated with our treatment by their staff.)

Leads can be added to automated email funnels, which split test email subjects and content based on open and click-through rates to determine the most effective emails.

Reminders can easily be set up and managed in one place on a per-salesman basis.

Barnes POS

Point-of-Sale System

June 2011-May 2012

More Info

Overview

The Barnes Point-of-Sale system is designed to dramatically simplify and reduce the costs associated with restaurant POS systems. By using web-connected devices such as tablets and mobile phones coupled with an internal web server a POS can scale seamlessly with inexpensive hardware and be easily updated and managed.

In 2011, my brother–a mobile app developer–and I began development and created a minimum viable product with a touchscreen-optimized web GUI as well as a complementary Android application. The system provided a robust administrator interface for tracking hours worked, managing products and tickets, and viewing sales data.

The code later provided a foundation for the Vendevor ecommerce platform and development ceased in 2012. The demo site is no longer online but screenshots can be seen below.

Technologies Chosen

CakePHP 1.3 + MySQL - Rapid development framework easily deployed on internal LAMP servers within restaurants
Android - Mobile app provided order recording functionality for servers in a pocket-sized device
